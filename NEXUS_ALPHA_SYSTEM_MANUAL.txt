1. CORE PHILOSOPHY & ARCHITECTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Nexus Alpha is designed as a personal, hybrid trading terminal built specifically 
for the Indian Stock Market (NSE/BSE). 

The platform separates the trading layers into two distinct pipelines:
- DETERMINISTIC PIPELINE (Code-Driven): 100% mathematical, rule-based indicators, 
  open interest math, relative strength matrices, and structure detections. 
  These run locally on the client to ensure zero-latency accuracy.
- COGNITIVE PIPELINE (AI-Driven): Uses Anthropic Claude as an institutional 
  analyst layer to explain setups, evaluate cross-market risk variables, and 
  formulate structured commentaries.

2. PRE-MARKET SEQUENCE (09:00 - 09:15 IST)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Before the bell rings, the terminal aggregates global and institutional data to 
establish the daily market regime and trading bias.

A. GIFT Nifty Gap Estimation
   - The terminal pulls the live price of GIFT Nifty and compares it to the 
     previous close of the Nifty 50 Index.
   - It calculates:
     - Gap Points = GIFT Nifty Price - Nifty Spot Close
     - Gap Percent = (Gap Points / Nifty Spot Close) * 100
   - Thresholds:
     - > +15 Points: GAP UP (Bullish opening bias)
     - < -15 Points: GAP DOWN (Bearish opening bias)
     - Between -15 and +15: FLAT OPENING (Neutral opening bias)

B. FII & DII (Institutional Flow) Analysis
   - Foreign Institutional Investors (FII) and Domestic Institutional Investors 
     (DII) net cash and futures transactions are parsed from daily exchange files.
   - Long/Short Ratio: Computes the net active long contracts relative to short 
     contracts in Index Futures. A ratio > 1.2 indicates aggressive institutional 
     long positioning; < 0.8 indicates distribution and potential short traps.
   - Net cash inflows determine if institutions are active accumulators (buying 
     eq block shares) or distributors (selling eq block shares).

C. Opening Volatility & India VIX
   - India VIX (Volatility Index) is measured.
   - VIX Ranges:
     - VIX < 13 (LOW): Stable market conditions; breakout strategies have lower 
       follow-through; range retests and mean reversion are highly profitable.
     - VIX 13 - 18 (NORMAL): Balanced premium pricing; structural breakouts and 
       trend following operate with high predictability.
     - VIX > 18 (HIGH): Elevated structural risk; wider stop-losses are required; 
       options premium spreads are expensive; leverage must be reduced.
     - VIX > 25 (EXTREME): Option buying is highly risky due to IV crush; 
       hedged options selling is preferred.

D. Daily Market Regime Formulation
   - Combining the GIFT Nifty Gap, FII/DII cash flow direction, and India VIX, 
     the terminal classifies the day's starting market environment as:
     - TRENDING_UP (Bullish gap + FII buying + low VIX)
     - TRENDING_DOWN (Bearish gap + FII selling + rising VIX)
     - RANGING (Flat open + mixed institutional flows)
     - CHOPPY (High VIX + directionless opening ticks)

3. INTRADAY RADAR & STRATEGY ENGINE (09:15 - 15:30 IST)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Once the market opens, the terminal runs parallel scanning layers to find 
high-probability setups.

A. Smart Money Concepts (SMC) Engine
   The local client-side engine scans 5M/15M charts for structural breaks:
   - Order Blocks (OB): Areas of heavy institutional order blocks where a strong 
     impulsive move originated. Unmitigated OBs act as powerful magnetic zones 
     for entries on pullback retests.
   - Fair Value Gaps (FVG): Price imbalances created by single-direction candles. 
     Unfilled FVGs act as liquidity targets that the price naturally returns to fill.
   - Change of Character (CHOCH): Represents the first sign of a trend reversal 
     (e.g., price breaking past the last lower high in a downtrend).
   - Break of Structure (BOS): Signifies trend continuation (e.g., price breaking 
     and closing past the previous higher high).

B. Relative Strength (RS) Scanner
   - Compares the performance of all Nifty 50 stocks against the Nifty 50 Index.
   - RS Score = Stock Performance (%) - Nifty Index Performance (%)
   - Positive RS Score: Stock is showing outperformance (alpha). If Nifty falls, 
     these stocks drop less. If Nifty rises, these stocks surge aggressively.
   - Negative RS Score: Stock is showing underperformance. Ideal candidates 
     for short setups when index structure breaks downward.

C. Futures Open Interest (OI) Buildup Classifier
   Every constituent stock's futures contracts are classified in real-time:
   - LONG BUILDUP: Price Up + Open Interest Up (Institutional long accumulation).
   - SHORT BUILDUP: Price Down + Open Interest Up (Institutional short addition).
   - LONG UNWINDING: Price Down + Open Interest Down (Long positions booking profits).
   - SHORT COVERING: Price Up + Open Interest Down (Shorts rushing to exit).

D. Option Chain Walls & Heatmaps
   - Put-Call Ratio (PCR): Volume/OI ratio. PCR > 1.2 is bullish (strong put writing); 
     PCR < 0.8 is bearish (strong call writing).
   - Call Walls (Resistance): The call strike with the highest Open Interest. 
     Represents the ceiling price that option sellers are actively defending.
   - Put Walls (Support): The put strike with the highest Open Interest. 
     Represents the floor price where active option writers are adding support.
   - Max Pain: The strike price where option buyers would experience maximum 
     financial loss. Option sellers naturally pull the price toward this strike 
     at expiry.

4. THE CONFLUENCE SCORER (THE TRADING EDGE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rather than taking raw single-indicator signals, the terminal aggregates all 
modules into a single, unified "Confluence Score" from 0 to 100:

- SMC Structure Alignment (OB Retests + BOS/CHOCH): 30% Weight
- Options Chain Metrics (PCR + strike walls proximity): 20% Weight
- India VIX Trend Compatibility: 15% Weight
- Institutional Position Stance (FII/DII): 15% Weight
- Sector Rotation Momentum: 20% Weight

Confluence Grades:
- GRADE A+ (Score 85-100): High-probability setup. Strong alignment across structure, 
  derivative metrics, and sector momentum.
- GRADE A (Score 70-84): High-probability setup. Solid structure with minor VIX or 
  institutional friction.
- GRADE B (Score 55-69): Moderate setup. Requires tighter stop-losses.
- GRADE C (Score < 55): Low probability. Terminal recommends waiting or mean-reversion 
  strategies only.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. PRE-ALERT AND POST-ALERT WORKFLOWS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A. Pre-Alert Phase (Scanning & Triggering)
   - The radar continually scans candle logs.
   - An indicator anomaly is detected (e.g., stock hits a daily order block, 
     relative strength jumps into positive zone, and futures OI signals 
     a Long Buildup).
   - The Confluence Scorer runs to verify the grade is at least A or A+.
   - If qualified, the Terminal triggers an alert on the UI.

B. Post-Alert Phase (Execution & Strategy Setup)
   - **Signal Generation**: The terminal automatically calculates entry thresholds:
     - Entry Zone: Proximity of the Order Block or breakout level.
     - Stop-Loss (SL): Just below the invalidation level (last structural swing low).
     - Targets: 1:2 or 1:3 Risk-to-Reward ratio based on unfilled Fair Value Gaps 
       or Call/Put Walls.
   - **AI Analysis Dispatch**: The setup's data matrix is dispatched to the AI.
   - **Execution Tracker**: The trade is logged into the simulated replay ledger 
     to track performance expectancy, win rates, and profit factors.

6. CLAUDE MULTI-AGENT QUANT SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When a trade setup is dispatched, the terminal routes it dynamically based on the 
calculated Confluence Grade to manage API costs and optimize response speeds:

- GRADE A+ SETUPS: Dispatched to the premium model (e.g. `claude-3-opus-20240229`) 
  for deep-dive contextual, multi-variable structural evaluation.
- HIGH-GRADE SETUPS: Routed to `claude-3-5-sonnet-20240620` for quick, precise, 
  technical reviews.
- MODERATE SETUPS: Dispatched to the fast `claude-3-haiku-20240307` model to 
  minimize latency and optimize token budgets.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. AI BROKER DESK (KITE MCP CONVERSATIONAL DESK)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The conversational interface acts as a local Broker Desk.
- When you type a query (e.g. *"Show my portfolio positions"* or *"Which sector is 
  strongest?"*), the backend loads local files and settings (`settings.json`).
- If an API key is available, it queries Claude using lightweight, contextual 
  instructions to explain performance in structured institutional format.
- If no key is set, it falls back to a deterministic query parser that reads 
  current sector momentum and mock portfolios to provide identical, fast 
  markdown tables and analysis without database fees or cloud exposure.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. THE HISTORICAL REPLAY BACKTESTER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Allows traders to test strategies against past intraday session candles:
- ORB Strategy: Enters on 15-minute high/low breakout boundaries.
- SMC Order Block Retest: Enters on price pullback into major detected 5M order blocks.
- It returns complete statistics: Total trades, Win-Rate, Profit Factor (Gross 
  Gains / Gross Losses), and Sharpe Ratio expectancy.
